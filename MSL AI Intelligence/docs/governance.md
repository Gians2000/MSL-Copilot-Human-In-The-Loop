# Governance

## Intended Use

This project supports medical affairs intelligence, evidence generation strategy, and clinical trial ideation in thoracic oncology.

## Not Intended For

- Patient-specific diagnosis or treatment.
- Promotional claims.
- Final medical, regulatory, legal, or payer submissions without expert review.
- Automated decisions affecting patient care or access.

## Claim Discipline

Every output should distinguish:

- sourced claim;
- inference from sourced claim;
- strategic hypothesis;
- open question.

## Review Expectations

Before external use, outputs should be reviewed by:

- medical affairs or clinical development;
- biostatistics where endpoint or subgroup interpretation matters;
- regulatory or legal when claims may be externally disseminated;
- market access where reimbursement or HTA implications are discussed.

## Recency

Thoracic oncology changes rapidly. Any output involving current standard of care, guidelines, labels, pricing, reimbursement, competitor status, or congress data must be refreshed against current primary or authoritative sources.
