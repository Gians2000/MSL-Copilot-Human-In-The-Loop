# Scoring Rubric

Score each trial opportunity from 1 to 5 on each dimension. Use half-scores only if the user explicitly asks for finer granularity.

## Dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| clinical_unmet_need | 0.18 | Incremental or already well served | Meaningful gap in a defined subgroup | Severe gap with poor outcomes and clear patient burden |
| evidence_gap | 0.13 | Evidence already mature | Some uncertainty or underserved subgroup | Major decision-relevant evidence gap |
| biological_rationale | 0.10 | Weak or speculative | Plausible mechanism | Strong translational or biomarker logic |
| differentiation | 0.13 | Crowded, little distinction | Some differentiating angle | Clear and defensible scientific differentiation |
| feasibility | 0.11 | Hard to recruit or operationally fragile | Feasible with constraints | Practical population, endpoints, and execution |
| competitive_urgency | 0.09 | Low pressure | Active but not saturated | Time-sensitive, competitor threat or white space |
| access_relevance | 0.09 | Little payer or HTA relevance | Some value argument | Strong reimbursement, testing, or pathway relevance |
| narrative_power | 0.08 | Hard to explain | Explainable to expert audiences | Compelling cross-functional story |
| patient_value | 0.09 | Limited patient-facing value | Some quality or burden benefit | Direct survival, symptom, toxicity, access, or convenience value |

## Weighted Score

Weighted score on a 0 to 100 scale:

`sum(score_1_to_5 * weight) / 5 * 100`

## Priority Bands

- 80 to 100: high-priority trial concept.
- 65 to 79: promising concept that needs focused validation.
- 50 to 64: exploratory or evidence-building concept.
- Below 50: deprioritize unless a strategic constraint makes it necessary.

## SWOT Bridge

- Strength fit: the concept uses a known advantage of the product.
- Weakness mitigation: the concept avoids, contextualizes, or tests around a limitation.
- Opportunity capture: the concept occupies an unmet need or evidence gap.
- Threat response: the concept protects against competitor, guideline, access, or testing risk.

Always explain the score in prose. A numeric rank without rationale is not sufficient.
