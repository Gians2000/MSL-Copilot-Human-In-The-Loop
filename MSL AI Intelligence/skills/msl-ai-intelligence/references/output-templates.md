|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Claim in one sentence | Citation or link | Setting and biomarker | OS/PFS/ORR/DOR/safety/RWE | Why it matters | Limitation or uncertainty |

## Unmet-Need Card

- Need: one sentence.
- Population: disease, setting, line, histology, biomarker.
- Evidence signal: trial, RWE, guideline, access, or stakeholder signal.
- Why now: competitive, clinical, regulatory, or access trigger.
- Patient relevance: survival, symptoms, toxicity, convenience, testing, or equity.
- Strategic implication: what decision this should inform.

## Stakeholder Narratives

- KOL: emphasize scientific rationale, evidence gap, trial design credibility, and publication value.
- HCP: emphasize patient selection, practical decision point, safety, and how evidence could change practice.
- Patient or advocacy: emphasize burden, meaningful outcomes, access, quality of life, and understandable tradeoffs.
- Payer or HTA: emphasize population definition, comparative value, endpoint relevance, resource use, and uncertainty reduction.
- Internal medical affairs: emphasize evidence generation, insight plan, KOL engagement, and cross-functional utility.
- Business partner: emphasize differentiated value proposition, timing, risk, and strategic fit.

## Elevator Pitch

Use four sentences:

1. The clinical gap.
2. The evidence or rationale supporting the opportunity.
3. The proposed trial concept.
4. Why it matters now.

Avoid hype. Strong MSL narratives sound inevitable because the evidence spine is clear, not because the language is inflated.
