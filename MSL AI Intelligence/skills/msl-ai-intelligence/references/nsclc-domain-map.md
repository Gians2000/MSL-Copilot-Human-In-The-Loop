# NSCLC Domain Map

Use this reference when the request involves lung cancer settings, biomarkers, endpoints, or common unmet-need patterns.

## Disease Scope

- NSCLC: non-squamous, squamous, mixed or NOS histology.
- SCLC: limited-stage, extensive-stage, relapsed or refractory.
- Priority MVP scope: NSCLC, especially metastatic disease and biomarker-defined segments.

## Core Clinical Settings

- Early resectable: neoadjuvant, adjuvant, perioperative, minimal residual disease.
- Locally advanced unresectable: chemoradiation, consolidation, post-consolidation relapse.
- Metastatic first line: driver-positive targeted therapy, driver-negative chemo-IO or IO combinations, PD-L1 high IO monotherapy or combinations.
- Metastatic second line and beyond: post-IO, post-targeted therapy, post-platinum, ADC or novel combinations.
- Special populations: CNS metastases, elderly or frail patients, poor performance status, autoimmune disease, renal impairment, steroid use, never-smokers, low testing access.

## Biomarker Map

- Immune markers: PD-L1 TPS, PD-L1 CPS where relevant, TMB, immune gene signatures, STK11, KEAP1, LKB1, HLA or antigen-presentation alterations.
- Driver alterations: EGFR, ALK, ROS1, BRAF V600E, MET exon 14 skipping, RET, NTRK, HER2, KRAS G12C, KRAS non-G12C, NRG1.
- Resistance markers: EGFR C797S, MET amplification, ALK resistance mutations, histologic transformation, bypass pathway activation.
- Testing infrastructure: NGS availability, tissue adequacy, liquid biopsy use, turnaround time, reflex testing, regional testing rate.

## Endpoint Priorities

- Efficacy: OS, PFS, ORR, DOR, disease control rate, time to response, intracranial response, event-free survival, disease-free survival.
- Safety: grade 3 or higher AE rate, serious AE rate, immune-related AE, discontinuation, treatment-related death, dose modification, long-term toxicity.
- RWE: real-world OS, real-world PFS, time to treatment discontinuation, time to next treatment, adherence, utilization by line, testing completion.
- Patient-centered: symptoms, quality of life, functional status, steroid reduction, hospitalizations, treatment burden.
- Access: eligible population, label population, reimbursement constraints, HTA value drivers, managed entry agreement considerations.

## Common Unmet-Need Patterns

- Driver-negative, PD-L1 low or negative disease with modest durable benefit from current standards.
- Primary or acquired resistance to immune checkpoint inhibitors.
- Post-IO and post-platinum disease without a clearly dominant standard.
- EGFR-mutant or ALK-positive disease after targeted therapy resistance.
- CNS disease with limited durable intracranial control.
- Squamous NSCLC where targeted options and molecularly tailored strategies remain limited.
- Frail, elderly, ECOG PS 2, or comorbidity-rich populations excluded from pivotal trials.
- Regions or centers with incomplete biomarker testing, slow turnaround, or low NGS penetration.
- Safety tradeoffs that limit real-world persistence despite trial efficacy.

## Strategic Product Questions

- Where does the asset plausibly change the endpoint that matters most for this segment?
- Is the best value story survival, durability, safety, convenience, biomarker selection, sequencing, or access?
- Does the asset solve a trial-evidence gap, a real-world implementation gap, or both?
- Which stakeholder must believe the narrative first: KOL, clinical investigator, payer, patient advocate, or internal development leader?
